Morning · 09:00

Pastry, then the ramparts

Coffee and something from the oven before the heat builds — then up to the fort to let everyone run. Free, unstructured, open walls high over the bay. Park outside and walk in; give it about an hour.

  • Padaria do Gá
    The town's best pastel de nata — tucked round a corner with no sign. Or Natas D'Ouro for a rotating cast of flavours the kids can pick from.
  • Castelo de Sesimbra Free
    9th-century Moorish walls and towers above the harbour. Mostly unstructured — ramparts to roam.
With the little onesOpen stone with unguarded edges — carriers or a firm hand for the 1, 3 and 4-year-olds.
02
Midday · 12:30

Lunch on the harbour

Down to the fishing port for whatever came off the boats this morning — the row of seafood places below the castle, with a promenade and ice-cream stands for after.

  • Costa Nossa Polished
    Patio over the water, the day's catch, attentive service (priced for the view).
  • Lobo do Mar Classic
    Walk past the ice counter, point at your fish, eat it off the grill across from the marina.
Seating the groupA walk-in table for twenty-odd on a summer Sunday is a stretch — call ahead, or plan to split across two neighbouring places. Skip Rodinhas with the group: no bookings, long queue.
✦
Early afternoon · 14:00

The villa pause

Back to base for the nap window — pool, shade, regroup. This is the natural split, too: families with the youngest can settle in and rejoin straight at dinner, while everyone else carries on for the scenic loop out to Azeitão.

04
Late afternoon · 16:30

Over the Serra da Arrábida

The N379-1 climbs into the limestone ridge and drops toward impossibly clear water — one of the prettiest stretches of road in the country.

  • Miradouro do Portinho da Arrábida
    The cliff-top viewpoint. With a convoy and toddlers, take the view and skip the descent — parking down at the beach gets painful in peak summer, and the villa pool already covered the swim.
05
Azeitão · 17:15

Tiles & cheese

Into the village that gives the cheese its name, with a craft stop on the way to dinner — and the two things Azeitão is known for to carry home.

  • Leiveira Azulejos de Azeitão Hands-on
    A working hand-painted tile studio — about €13 to paint your own (ready the next day). Best for the 4, 6 and 7-year-olds, with a couple of adults on littles duty.
  • Queijo de Azeitão & tortas
    Pick up the soft sheep's-milk cheese — scoop the top, spoon it onto bread — and the rolled tortas to take back.
  • José Maria da Fonseca / Bacalhôa Adults
    For a splinter group: a cellar tour and tasting at either historic house.
Book aheadReserve the tile workshop as a group — it runs better with notice.
06
Evening · 19:30

Dinner at Casa das Tortas

The heart of the village, on Praça da República — where locals gather and the courtyard grill runs late.

  • Casa das Tortas Reserved
    Black pork, fish off the courtyard BBQ, local wine, and — of course — the cheese and tortas.

Before you go

Four things worth sorting in advance

  • Lunch. Call your harbour pick to seat the group, or plan two adjacent tables.
  • Tiles. Reserve Leiveira as a group so the workshop is ready for you.
  • Closed today (Sun). Café BEC, Sesimbra's specialty-coffee spot; Favela, in Setúbal.
  • Cars. Name a lead car and a meet point — the Arrábida road is narrow and Azeitão's village lots are small.

The times are a loose spine, not a schedule. The pause in the middle is the whole trick — it lets the day bend around naps and still land everyone at the same table by dinner.
