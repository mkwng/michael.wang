Itinerary · June 2026 · Three families

Two Days in Lisbon

A clean route through the city — modern waterfront, the medieval old town, the great monuments, and the design quarters. Curated to keep the little ones happy and the driving simple.

Each day moves the cars three times. Park once per zone, explore on foot, repeat.
Day 01 · East
Waterfront, Tile & the Old Town
3 car moves  ·  in over the Vasco da Gama bridge  ·  best Tuesday–Saturday
1
Car move 1
Parque das Nações
the aquarium morning

A sleek modern district on the river, built for Expo ’98: wide promenades, public art, splash fountains, and the best attraction in the city for kids. The easiest, lowest-stress way to begin.

ParkCentro Vasco da Gama mall garage — underground, huge, ~€1–2/hr, never full first thing. Five-minute walk to the water.
TimingArrive ~9:15 for coffee · aquarium first slot 10:00 · cable car + lunch to ~1:30.
  • Morning coffee — Zagora Brunch (opens 8:30, closed Tue) or Nina (opens 9:00): proper specialty coffee right by the cable car.
  • Oceanário de Lisboa — book the first time slot; ~2 hrs, built around one enormous central tank.
  • Telecabine — a short riverside cable-car ride (opens 11). A hit with the kids.
  • Lunch on the waterfront before moving the cars.
2
Car move 2
Museu Nacional do AzulejoOptional
Portuguese tile

Portugal’s signature blue-and-white tilework, housed in a former convent and ending in a huge tiled panorama of pre-earthquake Lisbon. Calm, uncrowded, quietly beautiful. This is the stop to drop if the group wants a slower day.

ParkEasy street parking on Rua Madre de Deus, well off the tourist core; pay via the EMEL ePark app.
TimingA quick stop, ~1 hr, early afternoon.
  • The permanent collection — and the Grande Vista de Lisboa, a 75-foot tiled cityscape.
  • Garden café — a shady reset before Alfama.
3
Car move 3
Alfama
the medieval old town

Lisbon’s oldest quarter: a steep maze of tiled houses, washing lines, viewpoints and gelato. Touristy around the castle and the main terraces, but one lane off it’s still a living neighborhood. Best in the late afternoon, once the bus crowds thin.

ParkDon’t drive in — the lanes are scooter-width. Leave the cars at the Chão do Loureiro garage by the Sé cathedral (it has a lift toward the castle + a rooftop view) and walk up.
Timing~3:30 onward · golden hour at the viewpoints · early dinner.
  • Miradouro das Portas do Sol + its garden kiosk — rooftops to the river; juice for the kids, a drink for you.
  • Castelo de São Jorge (optional) — ramparts, peacocks, the widest view in the city. A climb; skip if the kids are flagging.
  • Cortiço & Netos — fourth-generation azulejo dealers selling discontinued tiles; they ship.
  • Gelado de Alfama / Amorino — the gelato stops.
  • Dinner — Prado (Baixa, farm-to-table, walkable) or Cervejaria Ramiro (loud, fast, legendary seafood — surprisingly kid-proof).
Day 02 · West
Monuments, Makers & Downtown
3 car moves  ·  in over the 25 de Abril bridge  ·  not Monday (Jerónimos closed)
1
Car move 1
Belém
the monuments — kept light for little ones

Postcard Lisbon on the river: the great monastery, striking modern architecture, gardens, and the original custard tart. We’ve kept this to one indoor stop and plenty of open space so the 3- and 4-year-olds don’t burn out.

ParkCCB underground garage (Centro Cultural de Belém). Surface lots fill by mid-morning, so the garage is the safe bet — arrive ~9:30.
TimingCoffee 8:30 · monastery at opening 9:30 · outdoor MAAT + gardens + tarts to ~noon.
  • Morning coffee — The Folks Belém (opens 8:30): a real roastery two minutes from the monastery.
  • Mosteiro dos Jerónimos — go to the cloister at opening; it’s a single open courtyard loop (~30 min) the kids can move around in. The one indoor stop.
  • MAAT — seen from the outside: walk up and over the white wave-roof, then the riverside path. No second museum interior.
  • Gardens & riverfront — room for the kids to run. (Save your nata for Manteigaria later, or brave the Pastéis de Belém line if you must.)
2
Car move 2
LX Factory
the creative-industrial lunch

A 19th-century factory complex under the big bridge, reborn as a village of design shops, murals, cafés and a famous bookstore. Genuinely cool, easy with kids (open courtyards, room to roam), and the midday hinge between the monuments and downtown.

ParkThe on-site lot fills, so use a nearby Alcântara garage and walk in.
Timing~12:15–2:15 over lunch.
  • Lunch in the courtyard — lots of options; kids wander between them.
  • Ler Devagar — a cathedral-scale bookshop with a flying-bicycle sculpture suspended over the shelves.
  • Landeau — the legendary chocolate cake (the only thing they make).
3
Car move 3
Chiado · Bairro Alto · Príncipe Real
downtown

Lisbon’s elegant heart: shopping streets, historic cafés, a gilded church, craft shops and garden squares, all walkable uphill from one garage. The afternoon for browsing, coffee, and a sunset viewpoint.

ParkSABA Praça do Município or the Mercado da Ribeira (Cais do Sodré) garage. Park once; don’t drive Bairro Alto’s tiny streets.
Timing~2:45 onward · sunset at the miradouro ~6 · dinner after.
  • Afternoon coffee — Fábrica, Hello Kristof, or Comoba — your pick of the third-wave roasters (Hello Kristof closes 3:45; the others to 5).
  • Manteigaria — the pastel de nata you skipped in Belém, made fresh and warm.
  • A Vida Portuguesa · Loja da Burel · Loja das Conservas — genuine Portuguese craft: goods, Serra da Estrela wool, design tins.
  • Livraria Bertrand · Igreja de São Roque — the oldest bookshop in the world, and a plain church with a staggering gilded interior.
  • Jardim do Príncipe Real — playground and a giant cedar for the kids; EmbaiXada palace-mall on the square.
  • Miradouro de São Pedro de Alcântara — the golden-hour view over the castle and Baixa.
  • Dinner — Atalho Real (Príncipe Real, steak, family-friendly) or Senhor Uva (Estrela, natural wine, a more grown-up night).

Good to know

Vehicles — standard SUVs clear the height bars at every garage here; only a roof box would be tight (CCB Belém is the lowest at 1.90 m). For a 3-car caravan, share the garage address and meet at the entrance.

Parking & payment — EMEL ePark and Telpark handle street parking; most garages take tap-to-pay. Don’t leave bags visible in the cars.

From the south — if central parking on Day 2 feels stressful, park at Pragal (Almada) and take the Fertagus train across the bridge — a park-and-ride that skips downtown driving entirely.
