LX Factory

the creative-industrial lunch

A 19th-century factory complex under the big bridge, reborn as a village of design shops, murals, cafés and a famous bookstore. Genuinely cool, easy with kids (open courtyards, room to roam) — a slow start to the day and the lunch stop before downtown.

  • Lunch in the courtyard
    Lots of options; kids wander between them.
  • Ler Devagar
    A cathedral-scale bookshop with a flying-bicycle sculpture suspended over the shelves.
  • Landeau One thing only
    The legendary chocolate cake (the only thing they make).
Market dayThe open-air LX Market runs Sundays only — on a Thursday or Friday you'll have all the shops, restaurants and the Ler Devagar bookshop, just without the market stalls (and far easier parking and smaller crowds).
Getting hereEasiest as a hop on the 15E riverside tram from Cais do Sodré (~10 min). If you do drive, the on-site lot is small — use a nearby Alcântara garage.
Timing~12:15–2:15 over lunch.

On foot· ~2:45 onward

Chiado · Bairro Alto · Príncipe Real

the design-and-craft afternoon — then a sunset

Lisbon's elegant heart, and the afternoon to actually shop it: the great Portuguese houses — porcelain, soap, tiles, wool, gloves — strung along a few walkable streets in Chiado, the concept palaces of Príncipe Real, a wine bar, and a viewpoint to close. The trick with five kids: ride up to Príncipe Real and walk the whole thing back downhill to the car — gravity does the work.

Good to know

For the west-side day

  • Belém skipped. The grand-monument box is ticked by the Castelo de São Jorge on Day 1, which keeps this an easy half-day.
  • From the south. If central parking feels stressful, park at Pragal (Almada) and take the Fertagus train across the bridge — a park-and-ride that skips downtown driving entirely.
  • Parking & payment. EMEL ePark and Telpark handle street parking; most garages take tap-to-pay. Don’t leave bags visible in the cars.
  • Don’t drive Bairro Alto. Park once at Praça do Município or Cais do Sodré and walk the hills.

The times are a loose spine, not a schedule. Park once per zone, explore on foot, and let the day bend around naps and gelato.

Getting around · Lisbon Day 2

Parking & getting around

The day spans Alcântara (LX Factory) and downtown (Chiado, Bairro Alto). The trick: park once, then let the tram, taxis and your feet do the rest.

The simplest plan

Park once at Cais do Sodré

One riverside garage downtown does every job — and you never drive Bairro Alto's lanes.

  • Why here Recommended
    Central and on the river: the 15E tram out to LX Factory leaves from here, Chiado and Bairro Alto are a short walk or the Bica funicular up, and Av. 24 de Julho runs straight onto the 25 de Abril bridge home. Park in the morning, collect after dinner — no second car move.
  • Mercado da Ribeira / Time Out Market
    Structured parking right at Cais do Sodré, beside the tram and the river. Takes cards, handles three cars.
  • SABA Praça do Município
    A few minutes east, large and reliable — the fallback if the riverside garage is full.

Out to LX Factory

The riverside tram

Make LX a tram excursion, not a parking fight — especially on a weekday, when it's calm.

  • Tram 15E Kids love it
    Cais do Sodré → Calvário (the LX Factory stop) in about 10 minutes, every ~15, 7am–11pm. The fun way west along the water. Tap a Navegante card or buy on board.
  • Uber / Bolt
    ~10 minutes and easy if the tram's packed — two cars for the group of eleven.
  • Driving & parking at LX Not the anchor
    Doable, but the on-site lot is small and the overflow is competitive street parking — worst on Sundays for the LX Market. On a Thursday or Friday it's manageable; even so, the tram saves the headache with three cars.

Around downtown

On foot, with a lift up the hill

  • Walking Most of it
    Chiado's craft shops are strung along a few flat-ish streets — do them on foot, at gelato pace.
  • Ascensor da Bica
    The little funicular up from the river into Bairro Alto — a landmark and a leg-saver on the steepest climb.
  • Elevador de Santa Justa Skip the queue
    The wrought-iron lift is photogenic but a long tourist line — the same view is free if you walk up to the Carmo terrace instead.
  • Tram 28 Iconic, crowded
    Passes through, but jammed and pickpocket-y — more sightseeing than transport.
  • Taxi / Bolt
    For the uphill hops to Príncipe Real, or out to dinner in Estrela.

The drive home

Straight onto the bridge

  • Av. 24 de Julho → 25 de Abril
    From Cais do Sodré the riverside road feeds right onto the bridge — minutes from car to crossing, the same clean exit as Day 1. Tolled northbound only; the rental's Via Verde tag handles it.

Skip downtown driving

Pragal park-and-ride

  • Fertagus from Pragal
    If central parking feels stressful, leave the cars at Pragal (Almada) and take the Fertagus train across the bridge into Cais do Sodré — no downtown driving at all, and you're dropped right at the tram and the walk up.

Apps & payments

Worth having on a phone

  • Uber & Bolt
    Both work well across Lisbon; Bolt is usually a little cheaper.
  • Carris / Navegante
    For the trams and funiculars — tap a Navegante Occasional card or buy on board.
  • EMEL ePark / Telpark
    Pay-by-app for street parking if a garage is full.
  • Tolls (Via Verde)
    The bridge is tolled; the rental almost certainly carries a transponder, so you just drive the green lanes.

The short version: park once at Cais do Sodré, ride the 15E out to LX Factory and back, walk the Chiado hills, and roll onto the bridge after dinner.
