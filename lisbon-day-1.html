Castelo de São Jorge

a castle to roam — not a maze to march

Straight up to the castle, and nothing else in Alfama: that keeps the morning to one contained place the kids can actually run, instead of the steep tourist maze. Ramparts and cannon to climb, peacocks in the gardens, a camera obscura, and the widest view in the city — an hour and a half of roaming before the heat builds.

  • The ramparts & camera obscura
    Walls and towers to climb, cannon to clamber on, and a periscope that pans live over the city.
  • Peacocks & the gardens Kids
    Shaded paths and lawns where the 1, 3 and 4-year-olds can just be let loose.
Getting upThere's no parking at the gate, and the lanes are scooter-width. The easy version: leave the three cars flat at the riverside Campo das Cebolas garage and take tuk-tuks or an Uber the rest of the way up.
TicketsBook castle entry online to walk past the queue. ~1.5 hrs is plenty before lunch.

Lunch on the way out

easy lunch — and the Portuguese-goods browse you wanted, two doors on

Down off the hill for a proper sit-down before the drive across town — somewhere gentle for the kids and not all fish. A family-run Mozambican kitchen for the pick, a cosy café for the simplest version, or the castle-side terrace if you mostly want the view.

  • Cantinho do Aziz Our pick
    A 35-year Mozambican kitchen in Mouraria, just north of the castle and on the way to the aquarium — roast chicken, mild curries and bread the kids will actually eat. Family-run and gentle. Book ahead.
  • Pois Café Easiest
    A cosy, sofa-strewn café by the Sé on the walk down — soups, sandwiches, salads and good cake. Walk-in and low-key, the simplest call with five littles.
  • Chapitô à Mesa For the view
    A terrace over the whole city at a circus school, where the kids can watch acrobats train — go for the setting more than the menu, which leans Portuguese and a touch seafood-y.
  • A Vida Portuguesa After lunch
    The one you wanted — and its big Intendente store is two minutes from Aziz, right on the way to the car: soaps, tinned fish, Bordallo ceramics, notebooks. A 15-minute browse. (The Chiado flagship is also on Day 2.)
RealisticallyYou won't sit down before ~1pm — a 10am roll-out with three cars, the drive and the bridge, parking, the lift up and a proper castle with five kids all add up. Plan around it rather than fight it.
ReserveBook Aziz or Chapitô ahead for the group; Pois is walk-in. Keep lunch to about an hour so the aquarium keeps its window.

The aquarium, then the bridge

Parque das Nações — and the way home

Across the city to the modern riverfront and the best thing in Lisbon for kids: the Oceanário, built around one enormous central tank you circle twice, high and low. Best of all, Parque das Nações sits right at the foot of the Vasco da Gama bridge — so the moment you're done, you're already pointed home.

  • Oceanário de Lisboa Book ~2:30
    Reserve a ~2:30 slot so you walk straight in — with the four o'clock exit, it's the squeeze of the day — a focused hour-and-a-bit around the great tank.
  • Telecabine If time
    A short riverside cable-car ride — only if the kids still have steam and the clock allows.
ParkCentro Vasco da Gama mall garage — underground, huge, a five-minute walk to the water.
Out by 4The bridge is right here: straight over the Vasco da Gama and roughly 40 minutes back to the house.

Good to know

A tight day, well run

  • The aquarium is the squeeze. Pre-book a ~2:30 slot — it's the one timed thing, and the 4pm exit is tight; reckon on about an hour and a quarter inside.
  • Just the castle. Alfama is kept to the Castelo de São Jorge alone — a contained romp for the kids rather than the steep, touristy maze. Park flat at riverside Campo das Cebolas and ride a tuk-tuk or Uber up; there's no parking at the gate. (Tap Getting around up top for every option.)
  • Reserve lunch. Book Cantinho do Aziz (or Chapitô, for the view) ahead for the group; Pois Café is walk-in. Keep it to an hour.
  • The clean exit. Parque das Nações is at the foot of the Vasco da Gama bridge, so you're out of the city within minutes of leaving the aquarium.

Leave by ten, home by four. One castle to run wild in, one great tank to gawp at, and a bridge waiting at the end of it.

Getting around · Lisbon Day 1

All our transport options

Three cars, six adults, five kids. In over one bridge, out over the other — and a flat-parking-plus-tuk-tuk plan for the castle hill.

Driving in & out

Two bridges, two jobs

Come in on one, leave on the other — it lines up neatly with the day.

  • Ponte 25 de Abril Going in
    The western suspension bridge drops you straight toward the old town and the castle. ~40 min from the house. Tolled northbound only — the rental's Via Verde tag handles it automatically.
  • Ponte Vasco da Gama Coming home
    The long northern bridge lands right at Parque das Nações — i.e. at the aquarium's doorstep. So you finish the day already pointed at it, cross, and you're ~40 min from home with no backtracking.

Parking by the castle

Park flat, don't fight the hill

There's no parking at the gate — Rua de Santa Cruz do Castelo is access-controlled — and the lanes up are scooter-width. Leave the cars somewhere flat and reliable.

  • Campo das Cebolas Our pick
    Modern riverside EMEL garage by the Sé and Praça do Comércio — clean, well-run, ~230 big spaces, takes cards. Tuk-tuks and Tram 28 wait right outside to ride up. The nicest place to start the morning with the kids.
  • Martim Moniz underground Flat, but gritty
    Closest flat garage to the gate and it swallows three cars, but go in clear-eyed: the garage is dated (old cash machines, tight ramps, ~2.3 m height limit) and the square reads rough — multicultural and lively by day, but with drug-dealer touts and best avoided after dark. Fine as a daytime backup if Campo das Cebolas is full.
  • Chão do Loureiro Not as close as it looks
    The hillside garage at Costa do Castelo with the free Baixa lift and a rooftop café. Handy, but the lift just lands you at its own viewpoint — still a ~10-minute uphill walk with steps to the castle gate. Fine for the view, not a shortcut.

The last stretch up to the gate

Riding up the hill

From flat parking, skip the climb with five littles — let something else do the steep bit.

  • Tuk-tuk Best with kids
    They wait all along the Baixa and the riverside and drop you right at the castle entrance. A hit with the little ones, and they take the gradient and the cobbles for you. Agree the price first.
  • Uber / Bolt
    Both run all over Lisbon and can drop at the gate. Two cars for the group of eleven. Often cheaper than a tuk-tuk and just as quick.
  • Bus 737
    The one public bus that climbs to the castle gate — from Praça da Figueira / Martim Moniz. Cheap and direct, but small and it fills up; tricky to keep the whole party together.
  • Tram 28 Iconic, but
    The famous yellow tram passes below the castle, but it only gets you to the base of the hill — still a climb — and the queues and pickpockets are real. More sightseeing than transport.
  • Elevador do Castelo Free
    A free public lift from Rua dos Fanqueiros in the Baixa up through Chão do Loureiro to Costa do Castelo (7am–9pm). Still leaves the same ~10-minute uphill walk to the gate — worth knowing, not the fast way.

At the aquarium

Parque das Nações

  • Centro Vasco da Gama garage Our pick
    The mall's underground garage is huge, easy with three cars, and a 5-minute flat walk to the Oceanário. Best of all it sits at the foot of the Vasco da Gama bridge — so leaving the garage and leaving the city are the same move.

Apps & payments

Worth having on a phone

  • Uber & Bolt
    Both work well across Lisbon — Bolt is usually a little cheaper. Handy for the castle hill and any "we're done walking" moment.
  • EMEL ePark / Telpark
    For paid on-street parking if a garage is full — pay by app rather than feeding a meter. Garages above take card at the barrier.
  • Tolls (Via Verde)
    Both bridges and the motorways are tolled. Rental cars almost always carry a Via Verde transponder — charges land on the rental — so you just drive through the green lanes.

The short version: 25 de Abril in, park flat at Campo das Cebolas, tuk-tuk up to the gate. Aquarium in the afternoon, park under the mall, and roll straight onto the Vasco da Gama bridge home.
